**Claude Fable 5 Is Back. Here Is What Happened**

If you tried accessing Claude Fable 5 over the past few weeks and ran into a wall, you were not alone. Millions of users worldwide hit the exact same dead end. And the story behind why that happened turned out to be far more dramatic than a routine outage or a scheduled maintenance window.
What unfolded over 18 days was genuinely one of the most talked about moments in recent AI history. A newly launched model, pulled offline within 72 hours of release, caught in the middle of a national security dispute between one of America's leading AI companies and the US government. Claude Fable 5 went from being celebrated as a major leap forward in publicly available AI to being switched off globally, almost overnight.
As of July 1, 2026, Claude Fable 5 is back. But the full story of what happened, why it was suspended, and what changed to bring it back is worth understanding properly. So here it is.

**What Is Claude Fable 5?**

Claude Fable 5 is Anthropic's most capable AI model made available to the general public. Built on what Anthropic describes as the Mythos class architecture, it was designed to handle demanding reasoning tasks, long form agentic work, software engineering, and advanced vision capabilities. When it launched on June 9, 2026, it was widely described as the most capable model Anthropic had ever released publicly, a meaningful step forward for anyone using AI professionally or building products on top of it.
Claude Mythos 5 is its counterpart. Same underlying architecture, but carrying enhanced safeguards built specifically for cybersecurity applications and sensitive workloads. Access to Mythos 5 was originally reserved for trusted partners through a programme Anthropic calls Project Glasswing, covering high stakes government and enterprise deployments.
Both models launched to real excitement. Neither stayed available for long.

**Why Was Claude Fable 5 Suspended?**

Three days after launch, on June 12, 2026 at 5:21pm ET, the US Department of Commerce issued a directive to Anthropic ordering the suspension of both Fable 5 and Mythos 5 for all foreign nationals, whether inside or outside the United States. That included Anthropic's own employees who are foreign nationals.
According to reporting from CNBC, Forbes, Axios and others, the concern centred on a potential security vulnerability. Amazon researchers had reportedly claimed that a specific sequence of prompts could lead Fable 5 to produce information that might be useful in cyberattacks. This kind of vulnerability is commonly referred to as a jailbreak, a method of bypassing a model's safety guardrails through carefully constructed inputs.
Anthropic disputed the severity of the finding publicly. The company argued the vulnerability was narrow and non-universal, and that the same standard applied across the AI industry would effectively bring frontier model deployment to a complete halt. Despite this, given just 90 minutes to comply with the directive, Anthropic chose to take both models entirely offline. The company explained that filtering access by nationality in real time was technically impossible to implement across its platforms, so disabling the models globally was the only viable path to compliance.
The disruption was immediate. Enterprise clients across finance, healthcare, software, and critical infrastructure lost access without prior warning. Developers building on Claude through Amazon Web Services, Google Cloud, and Microsoft Foundry were all affected. Claude Fable 5 was unavailable across every surface simultaneously

**What Happened During the 18 Days It Was Offline?**

The period between June 12 and June 30 involved what multiple outlets described as a lengthy negotiation process between Anthropic and the US government.
The suspension drew significant attention from across the technology industry. A number of tech executives and investors raised concerns publicly, with part of the criticism focused on a timing issue that made the restrictions particularly sensitive. Chinese open source AI developers had been making rapid progress in closing the capability gap with leading US models, and the argument from several quarters was that restricting Anthropic's most advanced public model while that competition was accelerating was working against US interests in AI leadership rather than protecting them.
A coalition of information security leaders published an open letter calling for the controls to be lifted, arguing that AI regulation should be grounded in scientific evaluation and democratic transparency rather than unilateral directives. That public pressure, combined with ongoing negotiations, gradually shifted the situation.
On June 26, Commerce Secretary Howard Lutnick granted Anthropic permission to restore access to Mythos 5 for a select group of US organisations, specifically those operating and defending critical infrastructure. It was a partial step forward. Claude Fable 5, the model most users had been waiting for, remained offline.

**Is Claude Fable 5 Back? Yes. Here Is What Changed**

On June 30, 2026, Anthropic announced on X that the US Department of Commerce had officially lifted export controls on both Claude Fable 5 and Mythos 5. In a formal letter to Anthropic's Chief Compute Officer Tom Brown, Commerce Secretary Lutnick confirmed that a licence is no longer required for the export, re-export, or transfer of either model.
Access restoration began on July 1, 2026, which is today.
As part of the agreement reached with the government, Anthropic confirmed it had worked to implement a new safeguard specifically targeting the vulnerability that prompted the original concerns. According to Axios, this safeguard blocks the relevant jailbreak approximately 99 percent of the time. Anthropic also agreed to proactively detect and address security risks going forward, to work with the government on protocols for future model releases, and to report any malicious activity it identifies within its models.
Commerce Secretary Lutnick stated publicly that his department had spent the previous two weeks working closely with Anthropic to analyse and approve Fable 5, describing the collaboration as a step toward strengthening US leadership in AI.
